Fraser syllables without a written vowel have the implicit vowel /É‘/. Syllables without a written consonant have the implicit initial consonant /Ê”/, except for /É¯/ and /É¤/ which have the implicit inital consonant /É£/. In Fraser Ë indicates the syllable /É‘/ with no implicit initial consonant. Initial /hÌƒ/ causes nasalization in the following vowel, it may also be caused by initial /Ê”/ or found in loan words. Nasalized vowels are indicated in pinyin by an n after the vowel, and may be indicated in Fraser by Ê¼ after the vowel.
